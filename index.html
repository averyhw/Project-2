Reduce, Reuse

Recycle

You can dramatically reduce waste and energy every day by recycling and buying products made of recycled materials. Small changes to your daily routine can make a large change in the amount of new materials gathered for production and the overall human footprint on earth.

Our Impact

In just the U.S. alone, over 258 million tons of trash are created every year. Recycling can reduce this waste dramatically and it might be easier than you think...

Collecting

curb-side

Simply place the recycled material in its designated bin and leave it on the curb with your trash collected by the city — it's that simple. Make sure to seperate your trash and recycling as you go to make it even easier.

drop-off

Your recycled materials can be dropped-off at your local designated drop-off location. This makes it easier for larger quantities of recyclable material and allows you to ask questions about specifics.

deposit location

Your recycling can be returned to a deposit center where one could recieve a few cents per item. For example, a plastic bottle could be worth up to five cents. It will add up over time and save materials — win, win.

The Impact

A lot of progress has been made over the past few decade in terms of amount of trash recycled.

In 1980, the average person produced an average of 3.7 pounds of trash per day, but less than 10 percent was recycled.

In 2014, the average pounds of trash per person per day increased to 4.4, but the percentage recycled increased to 34.6 percent (EPA).
